Deepening Mathematical Understanding

How should I handle students' "right" answers?

As teachers, we typically spend more time preparing to handle "wrong" answers than we do the "right" ones. Should a correct response signal that our work is done or are there other opportunities to consider? In this conversation, you'll have an opportunity to dig into different ways to respond when students give correct responses or demonstrate correct work.

A teacher considers a variety of ways to respond to a student's right answer.

  • What do you think is likely to happen next after each of the teacher’s responses?
  • What do these six responses communicate about who owns the expertise and responsibility for making meaning in the classroom?
  • When do you think each approach is best? How might these responses affect the class, beyond that single student?
  • What goals/intentions might a teacher have when responding to a “right” answer?
  • In your own practice, how do you tend to respond to “right” answers? Which of the approaches do you rarely use?
  • Are there ways of responding to "right" answers you’d like to do more of in your own practice? What challenges will you need to overcome to do this?
  • To prepare to respond to students “right” answers while teaching, what could you think about or do during your lesson planning?
  • What is one small thing you can do tomorrow in support of those shifts?
