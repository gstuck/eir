Deepening Mathematical Understanding

How should I sequence and share student work?

Have you ever wondered what to do when students solve a mathematical problem in different ways? Should you highlight only the most formal approach — or share multiple ways, and in what order? In this conversation, you'll consider the strengths of common mathematical representations, strategies for sequencing them, and the benefits of connecting them.

  • How are students solving the problem differently?
  • What are some strengths and weaknesses in each representation?
  • If you were teaching this lesson, which approaches would you want to surface to the whole class for discussion? If you choose to surface multiple approaches, in what order would you want to present them?
  • What connections would you want to help students make among the approaches — and how would you help them make those connections?
  • What does sequencing and sharing work look like in your classroom now? When you do this, what challenges do you experience?
  • Are there any shifts you'd like to make to the way you approach sequencing and sharing student work?
  • To prepare to meaningfully sequence and share student work while teaching, what could you think about or do during your lesson planning?
  • What is one small thing you can do tomorrow in support of those shifts?
