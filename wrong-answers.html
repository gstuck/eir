Deepening Mathematical Understanding

How should I handle students' "wrong" answers?

How can we turn “mistakes” or missteps into positive experiences when students are using math to understand the real world? Just as important as a mistake itself, is how we as teachers respond to it. In this conversation, you'll have an opportunity to explore different ways to respond to "mistakes" and discuss how those responses might support or undermine your goals.

Spark Artifact · Animated Video

One teacher reacts to a student's incorrect answer.

  • What goals/intentions might a teacher have when responding to a “wrong” answer?
  • What do you see happening in the animated scenario? What effects (positive and negative) do you anticipate the teacher’s reaction will have on what happens next?
  • What else could the teacher have said and/or done in response to this student? How do you expect this would have played out differently?
  • In what ways can a "wrong" answer support learning both for the student who gave it and for others?
  • Beyond the immediate response, what other classroom practices communicate a teacher’s expectations for mistakes and "wrong" answers? (Both intentional and unintended, explicit and implicit.)
  • Consider how you tend to respond when a student solves a problem incorrectly. How do you respond and why?
  • Are there any ways you don’t typically respond to "wrong" answers that you would like to try?
  • How might you prepare your students for any shifts in classroom culture and expectations around “wrong” answers?
  • What is one small thing you can do tomorrow in support of those shifts?
